From Sketch to Design

01 Fish & Lilypad

This sketch was based off of a reference photo I took in San Diego, California at Balboa Park. At the park, there is this large pond that is filled with lily pads and swimming underneath are koi fish. Balboa Park is one of my favorite places to visit in California and I especially like seeing this pond. In my sketch, I wanted to show the different layers of the pond and the disbursement of lily pads and flowers. Beneath the plants are these bright and colorful koi fish that always catch my attention. To create the layering effect, I used the multiply tool to have the intersection between elements stand out.

02 Fossil

This sketch was based on a few pressed flower images I saw online. I love saving fresh flowers and special moments in my life to hold onto the memory. This led me to thinking about fossils and how there is more focus on drawing fossils of bone skeletons rather than plant fossils. The fossil in my design is pink and red where the plant may have been pressed into a material like clay.

03 Conversation Hearts

This sketch is one of my favorites because I made the design as a way to almost mock the idea of Valentine’s Day. This sketch was drawn about a week before Valentine’s Day as I was thinking about all the rejection and break ups that occur before the day. The conversation hearts are usually used to spread positive and cute messages of “Be Mine” or “I Love U.” My interpretation puts a twist on the “Be Mine” heart, where it shows a conversation where someone is rejected and accepts the message. This design is more humorous rather than sad, as it is making fun of the idea of giving someone these conversation hearts for their intended purpose.

04 Heart Lollipop

The red heart shaped lollipop is very nostalgic for me because it reminds me of celebrating Valentine’s Day in elementary school. Valentine’s Day was a big holiday when I was little because everyone in my school classes would decorate boxes and bags to collect valentines from everyone in the class. This is the only time I truly celebrated Valentine’s Day, and I would always look forward to getting these lollipops. For some reason I never bought the lollipops or knew where they were sold, so it was always a treat I would be gifted from someone else.
