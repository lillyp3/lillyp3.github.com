Final Project Brief

Project Summary

I will be creating an online shop where the style of the website is inspired by the design of the items/clothing.

Comparison Project 1

Zara is a clothing store that uses an editorial and high-end fashion approach for the style of their website. I like how when you first visit the page, there is a short slideshow where you can browse each section of the website (mens, womens, or kids clothing). The slideshow uses a smooth scroll feature where it moves fast then slows down to set the picture on the screen. Additionally, I like the styling of the content that corresponds with each photo of the item. A picture for a pair of jeans had the title “denim” in an interesting font overlapping the photo, while the rest of the information was listed below in a simpler font and style. Finally, there is an interesting use of layouts for the items, such as having three pictures fill the width of the screen and have the second space left empty, while the next row only has two photos side by side.

Comparison Project 2

I like the timing of the interactive animations on Timothée Roussilhe’s website. As you scroll down different parts of the page move onto the screen from different directions at different speeds. For example the title and short text blurb move onto the screen from opposite sides, while the picture beside them floats in. Also, the photos have a timed overlay where you don’t need to hover over the photo, but as you move down the screen the overlay moves up to reveal the photo. Additionally, there is a number on the right that changes based on which section you are on. The transition resembles an analog clock that rotates to reveal the next number.

Project Goals

Similar to the websites mentioned above, I plan to create an online shop experience that has personality rather than a list of items to look at in basic rows and columns. I want to create additional small design graphics and assets to place on top of some of the photos. I want to use a variation of styles for the rows of photos, and integrate a small slideshow at the beginning when first opening the webpage. Some photos will take the user to a separate page dedicated for that design, and have more information on the product. When displaying the photos in the main page, overlays will be used to expose certain items as you scroll down.

Design Strategies

Colors for the website will be taken directly from the designs of the items in the photos. Most of the text will be in a thin and small dark colored font, to place more attention to the photos. The title of each design or item may stand out in a larger size and elaborate font. Photos with the same design (either fish, fossil, hearts, or lollipop) will be grouped together in various combinations, there will not be one set style of row or column. More space is placed between the different designs. Overlays for the photos will have the same color and font for any text on top.

Interaction Strategies

Most of the slideshows will be time based. Like my original studio project, I want a few images to continually change within the same spot. This slideshow could either show different color combinations or arrangements of the same item. There will be no explicit buttons to get to additional pages within the website, but rather the user can click the title or photo of the item. I may use a transition in the size or opacity of these features in order to make it easier to understand that those items are interactive. I will utilize a smooth scrolling effect, especially when clicking on an icon that leads to a portion of the page that is further down. Also, I will use a smooth scroll for the initial slideshow when looking through a general overview of the designs on the page.

Experience Goals

The experience I hope to achieve is an online shop with a gallery of different items to look through. The goal is to showcase each unique design on as many items as possible. Additional illustrations and graphics in the same style and colors of the design will be used to unify each specific collection of photos and give the user a better idea of what the designs represent. The user can go to specific pages for each of the 4-5 designs to learn more about the artwork. Also, there will be information about the products such as size and materials. I want the user to enjoy looking through all the items like a gallery show rather than provide an experience that pushes them to look for a specific item.
